# Market Assessment — "Gruntownie o nieruchomościach" ebook brand
**Prepared for:** Phil Parry (advisory to Magdalena Różanowska)
**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Researcher:** Fable (web-sourced; all Polish sources translated inline)

---

## Verdict: RESHAPE

The niche is real and Magda's core claim — almost no *paid, seller-side* materials exist — largely holds up. But the business as currently designed (organic social audience → passive sales of a 399–499 PLN PDF) is misaligned with how this audience actually behaves. Land sellers are **episodic, search-driven buyers**, not followers. The product set, pricing ladder, and channel plan all need restructuring before September's price rise locks anything in.

**Cheapest de-risking test:** a lead-magnet landing page ("what is your plot actually worth to a developer?") plus ~500–1,000 PLN of Google/Facebook ads against high-intent Polish search phrases ("jak sprzedać działkę deweloperowi" — "how to sell a plot to a developer"). Measure email captures and 149 PLN conversions over 30 days. That answers the demand question for less than the cost of writing a third ebook. An MVP of exactly this calculator page is built — see Phase 3 below.

## Phase 1 — Is there an actual market?

### 1.1 Addressable audience size

The raw transaction volume is large; the developer-facing subset is small but high-value.

- **GUS (Polish statistical office), "Property turnover in 2024":** 239,000 transactions in undeveloped land in 2024, worth 52.2 bn PLN — transaction count up 10.1% y/y, value up 23.9% y/y. Notarial deeds for undeveloped-plot sales rose 13.2% y/y. ([stat.gov.pl](https://stat.gov.pl/obszary-tematyczne/infrastruktura-komunalna-nieruchomosci/nieruchomosci-budynki-infrastruktura-komunalna/obrot-nieruchomosciami-w-2024-r-,8,8.html), via [Domiporta](https://www.domiporta.pl/informacje/a/ceny-mieszkan-rosna-ale-to-obrot-gruntami-przyspiesza-nowe-dane-gus-7652))
- **Ministry of Justice data for 2025** (via [kb.pl](https://kb.pl/aktualnosci/nieruchomosci/sprzedaz-dzialek-w-polsce/)): ~126,700 notarial deeds documenting sales of undeveloped plots (incl. building plots), plus ~65,700 for agricultural plots. Another summary of 2025 data puts undeveloped-plot transactions at ~203,000 of 586,600 total property transactions. The two figures use different category cuts; treat "roughly 130–240k undeveloped-land sales per year" as the honest range.
- **Developer demand is real and growing.** Large developers' land spending rose **53% y/y in 2025**; Archicom alone bought ~480 m PLN of land in 2025 and plans 600–700 m PLN in 2026; Dom Development spent ~600 m PLN in H1 2025. The largest dozen residential developers are collectively spending "several billion PLN a year" on land banks. ([propertynews.pl](https://www.propertynews.pl/tereny-inwestycyjne/deweloperzy-nie-oszczedzaja-na-dzialkach-wydaja-miliony-na-banki-ziemi,198207.html), [rp.pl](https://www.rp.pl/nieruchomosci/art42381071-banki-ziemi-deweloperow-na-jak-dlugo-wystarcza-zapasy-gruntow))
- **Land prices are rising faster than inflation** — residential development land up ~13% y/y in average locations, ~21% in prime locations in 2025; land is now 30–35% of the cost of building a flat. ([propertynews.pl](https://www.propertynews.pl/nieruchomosci-premium/ceny-gruntow-rosna-szybciej-niz-inflacja-w-warszawie-to-nawet-16-tys-za-metr,199110.html), [bezprawnik.pl](https://bezprawnik.pl/nawet-30-proc-ceny-mieszkania-to-koszt-samej-dzialki/))
- **Only ~32.5% of Poland is covered by a local zoning plan (MPZP)** (2024 figure; 31.2% in 2019 — it barely moves). ([teraz-srodowisko.pl / NIK audit](https://www.teraz-srodowisko.pl/aktualnosci/nik-kontrola-planowanie-przestrzenne-chaos-przestrzenny-13338.html), [blog.ongeo.pl](https://blog.ongeo.pl/pokrycie-mpzp)) This matters twice: it makes the subject genuinely confusing for owners (good for an educator), and it means two-thirds of owners are outside MPZP areas where much of Ebook 1's plan-reading content applies only partially (WZ decisions instead).

**Honest sizing.** Of ~200k annual undeveloped-land sales, most are small recreational/agricultural plots, family transfers, and plots sold to private individuals. The developer-facing subset — owners of plots in or near agglomerations with multi-family potential — is plausibly **low thousands of owner-side transactions per year**, concentrated around Warsaw, Kraków, Wrocław, Tricity, Poznań. The *considering-selling* pool is several times larger than the transacting pool, and each is a one-time buyer with six-to-seven-figure stakes. Conclusion: **a real but narrow, non-recurring market — viable as a lead-gen and premium-price niche, not as a volume ebook business.**

### 1.2 Competition — is "almost nothing for sellers" true?

**For paid products: essentially true. For free content: false.**

- **Free seller-side content is abundant but conflicted.** Searching "jak sprzedać działkę deweloperowi" returns pages of guides — but almost all are content marketing published by **developers themselves** (Rutkowski Development, Nowa Deweloper, Domino Park) or by agencies/intermediaries who monetize by buying or brokering the land ([atoffice.pl](https://atoffice.pl/jak-sprzedac-dzialke-deweloperowi-uniknij-najczestszych-bledow), [homeasset.pl](https://www.homeasset.pl/sprzedaz-gruntu-pod-dewelopera/), [commercialland.pl](https://commercialland.pl/uslugi/)). Translated example framing: "selling to a developer is one of the most effective ways to get an attractive price" — i.e., the counterparty writing the seller's education. This is Magda's opening: **no independent, seller-aligned paid product was found.**
- **Paid education is overwhelmingly buyer/investor-side.** Land courses exist — Symen24's plot-trading course, Grzegorz Kusz's "Kurs działkowy", Marcin Jarzyński's "Investing in land" online training, Hectares' land workshop, Sedpol's "Land trading specialist" course (1,690 PLN + 350 PLN exam) — all teach people how to *buy* or flip plots, not how an owner should sell one. ([symen24.pl](https://symen24.pl/kursy-nieruchomosci-katalog/), [sedpol.com.pl](https://sedpol.com.pl/specjalista-ds-obrotu-gruntami-pod-inwestycje/), [marcinjarzynski.pl](https://marcinjarzynski.pl/produkt/inwestowanie-w-grunty-szkolenie-online/))
- **No dedicated seller-side YouTube/TikTok channel found.** Polish real-estate YouTube is dominated by investor educators (Filip Kowarski — 50k+ people trained, flagship flipping course; Kuba Midel; Daniel Siwiec), all buyer-side. Searches for land-owner-education channels return gardening channels ("działka" also means allotment garden — a discoverability problem for Magda's SEO, worth flagging). ([filipkowarski.pl](https://filipkowarski.pl/opinie/), [vademecumblogera.pl](https://vademecumblogera.pl/kanaly-na-you-tube-o-inwestowaniu-i-nieruchomosciach-ktore-regularnie-sledze-i-polecam/))
- **The real competitor is "free":** the developer's own acquisition team, the commission-based intermediary, and free blog content all offer the seller "help" at zero upfront cost. Magda's product must be positioned explicitly against their conflict of interest.

### 1.3 Willingness to pay: 149–499 PLN for a PDF

- Mainstream Polish ebooks sell at **19–39 PLN**; pricing guides call this the "safe" self-publishing range ([refspace.com](https://b.refspace.com/ile-powinien-kosztowac-e-book/)). Expert/professional digital publications are a growing exception and are exactly the category generating self-publishing revenue ([restrukturyzacja24.com.pl](https://restrukturyzacja24.com.pl/czy-e-booki-nadal-sie-sprzedaja-analiza-rynku-ksiazek-cyfrowych-w-2025-roku/)).
- Polish real-estate **video courses** anchor at: 399 PLN (entry agent course, [lebiedz.com.pl](https://www.lebiedz.com.pl/kurs/kurs-agenta-nieruchomosci-online/)), 599–999 PLN (specialist courses), ~997–1,100 PLN (flagship agent course), 1,690 PLN (certified land-trading course, Sedpol).
- **Read-through:** 149 PLN (→199) for Ebook 1 is defensible for a niche expert PDF — above book pricing, below course pricing, trivially rational against a transaction worth hundreds of thousands of PLN. **399→499 PLN for Ebook 2 is course-tier money for PDF-tier format** — at that price Polish buyers get 12 hours of video with certificates elsewhere. The problem is format-price mismatch, not the number itself.
- Demographic caveat: land-rich owners skew older and rural/peri-urban; comfort with paying 400+ PLN online for a PDF from a small brand is unproven. No direct data found either way — this is exactly what the cheap test should measure.

### 1.4 Channel viability (FB/IG/YT/TikTok)

- No published growth benchmarks specific to Polish real-estate micro-creators were found; the general Polish influencer data shows a market where even top creators are shedding followers and attention is shifting to micro/nano creators ([sotrender.com](https://www.sotrender.com/blog/pl/2026/04/najwieksze-profile-na-instagramie-w-polsce/), [bemore.pl](https://bemore.pl/ranking-influencerow/)). Established real-estate educators (Kowarski et al.) built audiences over **years** of consistent output, selling recurring-interest products (investing) to audiences who stay.
- The structural problem is worse than the benchmark problem: **a plot seller is in-market once, for a few months, then gone.** They don't follow land-selling accounts for years; they search when the developer's letter arrives. Follower-accumulation strategies (TikTok especially) fit recurring-interest niches, not episodic ones.
- Implication: **search-intent channels beat follow channels here** — Google/SEO, YouTube search (long-form "how to" titled to match queries), and paid search/social against high-intent keywords. Social should be a trust layer (proof she's real and expert), not the acquisition engine. Realistic expectation for organic-only social in this niche: months of content to reach hundreds, not thousands, of *relevant* followers — too slow as the sole path to "a handful of 400 PLN sales a month."

### 1.5 English/German expansion

- **German audience (foreign owners/heirs of Polish land) exists but is served differently.** There is a visible German-language ecosystem around inherited Polish property — dedicated portals and German-Polish law firms publishing guides like "Inheriting a Polish property as a German" and "Selling property in Poland — 5 key points" ([immobilie-polen.de](https://immobilie-polen.de/als-deutscher-eine-polnische-immobilie-erben/), [rosepartner.de](https://www.rosepartner.de/rechtsanwalt-polen-immobilie-kaufen-haus.html), [anwalt.net.pl](https://anwalt.net.pl/erbschaft-in-polen/)). Their problems are inheritance certificates, land-register entries, and 19% income tax on sale within 5 years — mostly *legal-process* problems, solved by hiring bilingual lawyers, not by reading a negotiation ebook. The audience is small, high-value, and trust-buys services, not PDFs.
- **Buyer-side foreigners:** EU/EEA/Swiss citizens need no permit to buy most Polish property; non-EU buyers need an MSWiA (Interior Ministry) permit — 1,570 PLN fee, up to 2 months, area limits (0.5 ha for living needs) ([gov.pl](https://www.gov.pl/web/mswia/uzyskaj-zezwolenie-na-nabycie-nieruchomosci-akcji-udzialow-przez-cudzoziemcow)). A buyer-side English guide would compete with law-firm content marketing that is free and more authoritative.
- **Recommendation: no EN/DE editions now.** Translation is the cheap part; localisation of law/tax framing, separate trust-building, and separate channels are not. If ever revisited, German-first, and as a *service* lead magnet (short free PDF "Selling inherited Polish land from Germany" feeding cross-border brokerage referrals) — not as a paid ebook.

## Phase 2 — Strategy (demand is real enough to proceed, reshaped)

### 2.1 Positioning

**"The only advisor in the room who is paid by you, not by the buyer."** Every free competitor (developer blogs, land-buying intermediaries) has a structural conflict of interest. Magda should name this openly — it converts the crowded free-content landscape from a threat into her core differentiator. Secondary wedge: PUM-based valuation literacy ("developers don't buy square metres of land, they buy square metres of buildable floor area") — concrete, surprising, demonstrable in 60 seconds, and no free competitor teaches it honestly because it raises the seller's price.

### 2.2 Pricing

Current ladder is inverted and format-mismatched:

- **Keep Ebook 1 at 149→199 PLN** as the entry product. Fine as-is.
- **Do not sell Ebook 2 as a 399–499 PLN naked PDF.** Either (a) rebuild it as a "system": PDF + the templates as editable files + 2–3 short screencast walkthroughs + a checklist pack, sold at 399–499 PLN; or (b) keep it as a PDF at ~249 PLN and create a genuine premium tier: **bundle (both ebooks + templates) + 60-minute 1:1 consultation at 890–990 PLN.** The consultation tier monetizes the real asset (her expertise), prices against the 1,690 PLN course anchor, and is itself the agency funnel.
- Her no-discount stance is right for this niche (one-time buyers, price signals quality). The September price rise is a legitimate urgency lever — use it once, honestly.

### 2.3 Funnel: ebook buyer → agency lead

Proposed shape: **search/ad → free calculator or checklist (email) → 149 PLN ebook → premium bundle/consultation → agency mandate.** Each step self-selects for seriousness; a 400k PLN land mandate pays for years of ebook-level revenue, which is why the funnel framing matters more than passive-income framing.

**The credibility conflict is real and must be pre-empted, not hidden.** "Neutral seller educator" + "agent who wants your mandate" is the same conflict she accuses developers' content of — a sharp reader or competitor will point it out. Mitigation: (1) explicit disclosure page in every ebook ("I run an agency; this material is complete without hiring anyone, including me"); (2) frame the offer as a DIY / done-with-you / done-for-you ladder — education genuinely enables DIY, agency is the convenience tier; (3) keep the brand (Kanaan) formally separate from the agency, which is already her plan. Handled openly, the conflict becomes proof of expertise; handled silently, it's a landmine.

### 2.4 Franchise extensions (in priority order)

1. **Inheritance & co-ownership plots** ("Sprzedaż odziedziczonej działki / współwłasność") — the single most friction-laden seller scenario, evidenced by an entire German legal-content industry existing around it; equally painful domestically.
2. **The 2026 planning reform** — Poland's ongoing shift to *plany ogólne* (general municipal plans) is topical, confusing, and directly changes what owners' land is worth ([biznes.pap.pl](https://biznes.pap.pl/wiadomosci/firmy/obowiazek-planow-ogolnych-za-pol-roku-zmiana-pozadana-ale-problematyczna-opinia)). Short, urgent, newsjack-able ebook or paid webinar.
3. **Selling agricultural land** — KOWR pre-emption rights and buyer restrictions; ~65,700 agricultural-plot deeds in 2025 says the audience exists ([kb.pl](https://kb.pl/aktualnosci/nieruchomosci/sprzedaz-dzialek-w-polsce/)).
4. **Taxes on land sale** — natural short companion product.
Avoid: buyer-side content (crowded), EN/DE editions (above).

### 2.5 Flag: the Kanaan copyright notice

Both shipped PDFs state "Publisher and owner of economic rights: Kanaan Land Property Investment Sp. z o.o., © 2026" — **a company that does not yet exist.** Not a business blocker, but legal hygiene worth fixing before scale: a non-existent entity cannot own copyright, so the notice is currently false; rights sit with Magda personally and should be formally assigned to Kanaan on registration (and the PDFs re-exported). Trivial now, messy later — especially if the brand is ever sold or infringed.

### 2.6 Verdict detail

**RESHAPE, not GO,** because three load-bearing assumptions are unvalidated or wrong: (1) passive-income framing — the audience is episodic, so revenue is funnel-shaped, not passive; (2) 399–499 PLN for a naked PDF is off-market; (3) organic social as primary channel misfits a search-driven audience. **Not KILL,** because the niche gap is genuinely confirmed, developer demand (the demand behind her audience's demand) is booming, the products are already written, and marginal cost to test is near zero.

**The single cheapest test:** launch the calculator landing page (built — below), point ~500–1,000 PLN of Google Search + Facebook ads at "sprzedaż działki deweloperowi"-cluster keywords, 30 days. Success gates: ≥ 3–5% ad-click→email conversion and ≥ a handful of 149 PLN sales. That validates demand, price tolerance, and channel in one move — before September's price rise and before registering Kanaan.

## Phase 3 — MVP built

**Location:** `/Users/oganiko/OG/dev/OG-033-Magda-Ebooks/src/mvp-land-calculator/index.html` (single static file, no dependencies, not deployed).

"Ile deweloper naprawdę zapłaci za Twoją działkę?" — a Polish-facing lead-magnet page implementing the PUM math from Ebook 1: plot area × intensity ratio (from MPZP or estimated by area type) → buildable floor area (PUM) → × per-m²-PUM rate by location (anchored to reported market rates, e.g. Warsaw centre up to ~16,000 PLN/m² PUM, Warsaw non-central ~8,000 PLN — [propertynews.pl](https://www.propertynews.pl/nieruchomosci-premium/ceny-gruntow-rosna-szybciej-niz-inflacja-w-warszawie-to-nawet-16-tys-za-metr,199110.html)) → estimated value range. Result is email-gated (UI mock only, no backend). Dark-green palette per the fixed brand; disclaimer that it's an educational estimate, not a valuation.
