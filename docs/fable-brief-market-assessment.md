# Brief for Fable 5 — Market Assessment: "Gruntownie o nieruchomościach"

**Project**: OG-033-Magda-Ebooks
**Requested by**: Phil Parry (advisor to Magdalena Różanowska)
**Date**: 2026-07-16

## Context

Magda Różanowska is a solo real-estate agent in Warsaw (also the subject of `OG-017-Magdas-Website`, her personal-brand site). Separately, she has built an educational ebook brand, **"Gruntownie o nieruchomościach"** ("Thoroughly about real estate" — a pun on *grunt*, Polish for "land plot"), targeting **land plot owners** who are approached by property developers to sell.

She has written two ebooks so far (reviewed in full — see `research/` in this project's OG-Research folder):

1. **"Sprzedaj korzystnie i bezpiecznie grunt deweloperowi"** ("Sell your land to a developer profitably and safely") — 16 chapters across 5 modules: how developers value land (PUM/PUU — buildable residential/commercial floor area, the core valuation currency), reading local zoning plans (MPZP) and buildability indicators, site-prep pitfalls that let developers lowball, contract mechanics (LOI, conditional pre-sale agreements, cash-vs-barter deals), and negotiation/marketing tactics for reaching developer expansion teams directly. Includes templates: investor teaser, glossary, verification-question checklist.
2. **"Wyciśnij maksimum z działki"** ("Squeeze the maximum out of your plot") — 16 chapters across 6 modules, aimed at owners selling to any buyer (not just developers): document/title checks, zoning lookup, valuation methods, cheap ways to raise perceived value before listing, marketing/photography/copywriting for listings, transaction safety, and post-sale tax treatment. Includes templates: seller's document checklist, plot info-card, listing copy template.

Both are dense, technically specific, and genuinely aimed at an underserved angle — nearly all Polish real-estate content targets **buyers**; this targets **sellers of undeveloped land**, a much narrower and more legally/technically complex audience.

**Business model (per Magda, 2026-07-15 email):**
- Pricing: Ebook 1 at 149 PLN (→199 PLN from September); Ebook 2 at 399 PLN (→499 PLN from September). She will not discount.
- Channels: FB, Instagram, YouTube live; TikTok planned. Building content/audience before actively promoting the ebooks.
- Revenue model: ebook sales as passive income **and** as a lead-gen funnel into her real-estate agency.
- Structure: if sales validate, she intends to register a limited company, **"Kanaan"**, to hold the brand and ebook rights.
- **Note for Fable to flag back to Phil**: both ebook PDFs already print "Wydawca i właściciel praw majątkowych: **Kanaan Land Property Investment Sp. z o.o.**, © 2026" on the copyright page — i.e. the company is already named as legal publisher/rights-owner in the shipped product, ahead of being registered. Worth a light IP/compliance flag in the strategy output, not a blocker.
- Brand is fixed: dark-green palette + existing logo, not up for redesign.

## What Phil needs from you

**Phase 1 — Is there an actual market? (do this first, don't skip to strategy)**

1. **Addressable audience size across Poland** (not just Warsaw): how many individual landowners are realistically in a position to sell to a developer or on the open market each year? Use whatever proxies are findable — MPZP conversion rates, land transaction volumes, developer land-acquisition activity, GUS/statistical data, real-estate portal listing volumes for działki (land plots).
2. **Existing competition**: search for Polish-language books, courses, YouTube/TikTok channels, blogs, or paid consultations already serving landowners on the *seller* side of a developer negotiation. Is Magda's claim — "almost no materials for the selling party" — actually true, or is this a crowded niche she hasn't found yet?
3. **Willingness to pay**: find comparable Polish digital-product price points (real-estate/legal/financial education ebooks, courses) to sanity-check 149–499 PLN against what this audience (often older, land-owning, sometimes rural/peri-urban) will actually pay for a PDF.
4. **Channel viability**: realistic benchmarks for organic FB/IG/YT/TikTok growth in the Polish real-estate niche — how long and how much content it typically takes to reach an audience large enough to sell a handful of 400 PLN ebooks a month.
5. **English/German-language expansion value**: is there a real audience for English and/or German editions of this material — distinct from the core Polish market, sized separately? Two candidate audiences: (a) foreign owners/heirs of Polish land (often German, given proximity — and consistent with the "institutional capital moving into Mazowieckie" finding already on OG-017) who don't read Polish and need help selling; (b) foreign investors/buyers wanting the *buyer*-side context on the Polish land market. Don't assume translation is free upside — factor in localisation cost, different trust signals, different channels, and any legal caveats around foreign land ownership/sale in Poland.

**Phase 2 — If (and only if) Phase 1 shows real demand: strategy**

- Positioning/differentiation relative to whatever competitors you find.
- Whether the current two-tier pricing makes sense, or should change.
- Funnel design from ebook buyer → agency lead — and whether combining "neutral seller education" with "I'm also the agent who might represent you" creates a credibility conflict worth addressing.
- Which additional ebook topics (if any) would extend the franchise.
- Whether an English and/or German edition is worth building, based on the Phase 1 sizing — and if so, which one first.
- A view on whether this stays a standalone product (OG-033) or should fold back into Magda's main website project (OG-017) once scoped.
- A straight **GO / RESHAPE / KILL** call, with the single cheapest test that would de-risk it fastest.

**Phase 3 — Optional: simple MVP**

If Phase 1 supports it, build a small, single-purpose prototype that demonstrates what a fuller product/portal could become — not a full app. Best candidate: an interactive **"what's my land actually worth to a developer"** calculator, built directly from the PUM/MPZP math already in Ebook 1 (buildable floor area × per-m² rate → estimated land value), as a lead-magnet landing page. Keep it to one page, Polish-facing copy, email-gated result. Do not deploy to production — a local/preview build is enough to show the concept.
